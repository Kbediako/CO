# 1060 Deliberation - Standalone Review Meta-Surface Expansion Guard

## Why this slice exists

- `1059` fixed the bounded nearby-file low-signal seam.
- Real `1059` review evidence still showed the wrapper staying active by broadening into `~/.codex/memories`, `~/.codex/skills`, `.runs/**/manifest.json`, `.runs/**/runner.ndjson`, and nested review/delegation work.
- Real Symphony research confirmed the right direction is still one authoritative runtime owner plus thin wrapper/projection layers, not a more autonomous supervisor.

## Chosen seam

Bounded meta-surface expansion classification is the next smallest seam because it:

- reuses the shared `ReviewExecutionState` owner
- targets the specific live broadening behavior already captured in `1059`
- improves reliability without turning the wrapper into a semantic policy engine
- preserves CO’s advisory-only authority model

## Explicit non-choice

This slice does **not** attempt to:

- classify arbitrary reviewer prose as on-task or off-task
- add retries, restarts, or re-dispatch behavior
- copy Symphony’s issue/scheduler ownership
- solve every broader adjacent-file tangent in one change
